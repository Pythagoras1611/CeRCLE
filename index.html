Welcome to CeRCLE: Our Launch Story

Building a community where emerging scholars thrive together

CeRCLE began with a simple conversation between four graduate students during a residency at the National Humanities Center. What started as casual introductions quickly became something much deeper—a shared recognition of the hidden struggles that define so much of the graduate school experience.

An Unexpected Connection

The four of us came from different disciplines and universities. On the surface, our research couldn't have been more different. But as we began sharing our stories, we discovered something remarkable.

Despite our different fields, we were all carrying the same invisible weight.

"We realized that while our research questions were unique, the emotional and practical challenges of graduate life were remarkably universal."

The Unseen Burdens

As our conversations deepened, we began talking about the parts of graduate school no one discusses in orientation sessions or faculty meetings. The imposter syndrome that follows you into every seminar. The isolation of working on a project only three people in the world truly understand. The financial stress of living on a graduate stipend. The uncertainty about whether academia is really the right path.

We talked about the pressure to appear constantly productive, the guilt of taking breaks, the challenge of maintaining relationships outside academia. We discussed the difficulty of finding mentors who understand both the intellectual and emotional demands of graduate work.

As we shared these experiences, we had a collective realization: we had been trying to carry these burdens alone, thinking they were personal failings rather than systemic challenges that affect nearly every graduate student.

A Better Way Forward

That conversation changed everything. If the four of us, from completely different fields, were experiencing such similar challenges, how many other emerging scholars were struggling in isolation? How much easier would our journeys be if we had a community that acknowledged these struggles and provided real support?

We spent the rest of our residency imagining what such a community might look like. Not just another professional network, but a space where scholars could be vulnerable about their challenges, share practical skills for navigating graduate life, and offer genuine mentorship to one another.

From Idea to Reality

The idea wouldn't let us go: what if we could create the support system we wished we'd had from the beginning?

And so, CeRCLE was born—a Community of emerging scholars for Research, Collaboration, Learning, and Exchange. Built on the simple understanding that the burdens of academic life become lighter when shared, and that every scholar deserves a community that supports both their research and their humanity.

Join Our Story

Today, CeRCLE is that conversation extended to scholars around the world. We're still those four graduate students who met at the National Humanities Center, but now we're part of a growing community of emerging scholars who believe that supporting each other makes us all stronger.

If you're carrying unseen burdens in your academic journey, if you're looking for genuine community and mentorship, if you believe that scholarship is both an intellectual and deeply human endeavor—then CeRCLE is for you.

Welcome to our community. Welcome to sharing the load.
